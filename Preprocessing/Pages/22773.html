dcterms.abstractThis thesis uses discourse analysis to explore the Radical Left student movement in Iran as a social movement. It examines how this movement fell into dormancy and its aim is to reveal how the Radical Left modulated its discursive frames and its collective identity in the context of an authoritarian regime. It aims at identifying the elements that encouraged the mobilization of students and the groups with which it wanted to ally (workers and women). Comparison with the past struggle of the Iranian left against authoritarian regimes reveals the effects of the collective-action and discourse repertoire of these movements. This also makes it possible to call into question whether there was innovation in the repertoire thirty years after the 1979 revolution, and to identify the means used in support of the socioeconomic demands of Iranians. Discourse analysis of the Radical Left reveals a joint absence of innovation in its discursive strategies, cultural links, as well as the elements of the concrete demands by Iranians. Through analysis of twenty-seven articles published and distributed between 2006 and 2008, the analysis shows how the Radical Left used its inherited repertoire without innovation to construct its collective identity and its discursive strategies (‚ÄúFreedom and Equality‚Äù and ‚ÄúStruggle against Imperialism‚Äù). Although the struggle was conceived as directed mainly against the political regime, it is shown to have been in fact directed discursively against reformists and liberals. Indeed, the dynamism of this struggle, because it took a position close to that of the dominant media, was weakened.
